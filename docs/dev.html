Why Augmented Reality?

Research shows that traditional paper and 2D maps increase cognitive load, distracting students from their environment.

The Challenge

New students often find large campuses like UNLV overwhelming. Traditional 2D maps require users to mentally translate a flat image into 3D space.

  • High cognitive processing required
  • Disconnect from surroundings
  • Difficult for visually/intellectually impaired

The Rebel Reality Solution

By overlaying digital arrows directly onto the physical world through a phone camera, we reduce the mental effort needed to navigate.

  • Intuitive visual guidance
  • Real-time localization
  • Increased independence for all users

Research-Backed Design

Reduced Cognitive Load

AR navigation decreases the mental effort needed for wayfinding compared to traditional 2D maps, allowing users to focus more on their surroundings and discover new locations of interest.

Enhanced Independence

Studies show that individuals with intellectual and sensory disabilities achieve up to 100% unassisted navigational independence with AR navigation, compared to under 50% with traditional methods.

Spatial Awareness

Users of AR navigation display increased eye movement and freedom to explore their environment, making the campus experience more engaging and memorable.
